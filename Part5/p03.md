# 📘 Chapter 17: Transfer Learning

---

## 🧠 17.1 Why Do We Need Transfer Learning?

Training a deep CNN from scratch needs:

* Huge data
* Long training time
* High computing power

👉 But often we don’t have these.

---

👉 Solution:

> **Transfer Learning**

---

## 🔁 17.2 What is Transfer Learning?

Transfer learning means:

> Using a model that is already trained and adapting it to a new task

---

### 🧠 Real-life analogy

Imagine:

* You already know how to drive a car
* Now learning to drive a truck becomes easier

👉 You reuse previous knowledge

---

## 📦 17.3 Pre-trained Models

These are models trained on large datasets like **ImageNet**

👉 They already know:

* edges
* shapes
* objects

## 🔧 17.5 Two Ways to Use Transfer Learning

---

### ✅ 1. Feature Extraction

* Use pretrained model as it is
* Only change final layer

---

👉 Steps:

1. Load model
2. Freeze layers
3. Train last layer

---

---

### ✅ 2. Fine-Tuning

* Unfreeze some layers
* Train part of the model

---

👉 Gives better performance
👉 Needs more data

---

## 🧠 17.6 Why Transfer Learning Works

Because:

* Early layers learn **general features**
* These features work for many tasks

---

👉 Example:

* Edge detection works for:

  * cats
  * cars
  * X-rays

---

## 🧪 17.8 Transfer Learning in PyTorch

---

### Step 1: Load pretrained model

```python id="rgzzwy"
import torchvision.models as models

model = models.resnet18(pretrained=True)
```

---

### Step 2: Freeze layers

```python id="d0qmav"
for param in model.parameters():
    param.requires_grad = False
```

---

### Step 3: Replace final layer

```python id="q2n78n"
import torch.nn as nn

model.fc = nn.Linear(model.fc.in_features, 10)
```

---

### Step 4: Train only final layer

```python id="cz63c4"
optimizer = torch.optim.Adam(model.fc.parameters(), lr=0.001)
```

---

## 🧠 17.9 When to Use Transfer Learning

### ✅ Small dataset

### ✅ Complex problem

### ✅ Limited time

---

👉 Perfect for:

* Medical imaging
* X-ray classification
* Real-world applications

---

## ⚠️ 17.10 When NOT to Use It

* Very different data
* Very large dataset available

---

## 🔍 17.11 Transfer Learning vs Training from Scratch

| Feature       | From Scratch | Transfer Learning |
| ------------- | ------------ | ----------------- |
| Data needed   | Large        | Small             |
| Training time | High         | Low               |
| Accuracy      | Variable     | Often better      |

---

## 🧠 17.12 Key Insight

Transfer learning is like:

> Standing on the shoulders of previous knowledge

---

## 🎯 17.13 Key Takeaways

* Reuse pretrained models
* Saves time and data
* Works well for real-world tasks

---

👉 Most important idea:

> Don’t always start from zero — reuse what is already learned
